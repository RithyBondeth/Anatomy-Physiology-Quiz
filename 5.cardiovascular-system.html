Question 1 of 15
Which side of the heart carries oxygen-poor blood and serves the pulmonary circuit?
A Left side
B Right side
C Both sides equally
D Neither side
Explanation
The right side of the heart carries oxygen-poor blood and serves the pulmonary circuit, which transports blood to and from the lungs for gas exchange.
Question 2 of 15
What is the normal sequence of the cardiac conduction system?
A AV node → SA node → Purkinje fibers → AV bundle
B SA node → AV node → AV bundle → Purkinje fibers
C Purkinje fibers → AV bundle → SA node → AV node
D AV bundle → SA node → Purkinje fibers → AV node
Explanation
The correct sequence is: SA node (pacemaker) → AV node → AV bundle → bundle branches → Purkinje fibers. This pathway ensures coordinated contraction of the heart chambers.
Question 3 of 15
Which layer of the heart wall is the chief contractile layer and forms the bulk of the heart?
A Epicardium
B Myocardium
C Endocardium
D Pericardium
Explanation
The myocardium is composed mainly of cardiac muscle and is the chief contractile layer that forms the bulk of the heart. It is interlaced with elastin and collagen fibers.
Question 4 of 15
Which of the following is NOT one of the three sources of resistance to blood flow?
A Blood viscosity
B Vessel length
C Vessel diameter
D Blood temperature
Explanation
The three sources of resistance to blood flow are blood viscosity, vessel length, and vessel diameter. Blood temperature is not listed as one of the primary sources of resistance.
Question 5 of 15
What is the function of the intercalated discs in cardiac muscle tissue?
A Store calcium ions for contraction
B Produce ATP for muscle contraction
C Interlock membranes and assist in propagation of action potentials
D Protect the heart from overstretching
Explanation
Intercalated discs interlock cardiac muscle cell membranes and contain gap junctions that allow for rapid propagation of action potentials, enabling coordinated contraction of the heart.
Question 6 of 15
Which valve prevents backflow of blood from the aorta into the left ventricle?
A Tricuspid valve
B Mitral (bicuspid) valve
C Aortic semilunar valve
D Pulmonary semilunar valve
Explanation
The aortic semilunar valve prevents backflow of blood from the aorta into the left ventricle during ventricular relaxation.
Question 7 of 15
Which type of blood vessel has the thinnest walls and directly serves cellular needs through exchange?
A Arteries
B Arterioles
C Capillaries
D Veins
Explanation
Capillaries have the thinnest walls (composed of just endothelium and basement membrane) and directly serve cellular needs through exchange of gases, nutrients, and wastes.
Question 8 of 15
During which phase of the cardiac cycle do the atria contract while the ventricles relax?
A Atrial diastole
B Ventricular systole
C Atrial systole
D Cardiac diastole
Explanation
During atrial systole, the atria contract while the ventricles relax, allowing blood to flow from the atria into the ventricles.
Question 9 of 15
What is the function of the serous fluid in the pericardial cavity?
A Provide nutrients to the heart muscle
B Produce red blood cells
C Lubricate and reduce friction as the heart beats
D Store calcium ions for contraction
Explanation
The serous fluid in the pericardial cavity lubricates the heart and reduces friction as it beats within the pericardial sac.
Question 10 of 15
Which of the following is a key difference between cardiac and skeletal muscle?
A Cardiac muscle has gap junctions between cells, skeletal muscle does not
B Skeletal muscle has intercalated discs, cardiac muscle does not
C Cardiac muscle can undergo tetanus, skeletal muscle cannot
D Skeletal muscle has pacemaker cells, cardiac muscle does not
Explanation
Cardiac muscle has gap junctions between cells that create a functional syncytium, allowing coordinated contraction. Skeletal muscle lacks these gap junctions and contracts as individual motor units.
Question 11 of 15
Which type of capillary has oval pores in the endothelial cells and is more permeable to fluids and solutes?
A Continuous capillaries
B Fenestrated capillaries
C Sinusoidal capillaries
D Arterial capillaries
Explanation
Fenestrated capillaries have oval pores (fenestrations) in their endothelial cells, making them more permeable to fluids and solutes. They are found in organs like the kidneys and pituitary gland.
Question 12 of 15
What is the normal blood pressure reading in mmHg?
A 100/60
B 120/80
C 140/90
D 160/100
Explanation
A normal blood pressure reading is 120/80 mmHg, where 120 represents systolic pressure (during ventricular contraction) and 80 represents diastolic pressure (during ventricular relaxation).
Question 13 of 15
Which layer of a blood vessel contains circularly arranged smooth muscle and is the thickest layer in arteries?
A Tunica intima
B Tunica media
C Tunica externa
D Tunica adventitia
Explanation
The tunica media contains circularly arranged smooth muscle and elastic fibers. It is the thickest layer in arteries and is regulated by sympathetic nerve fibers for vasoconstriction and vasodilation.
Question 14 of 15
What is the role of the chordae tendineae and papillary muscles in the heart?
A Initiate the electrical impulse for contraction
B Prevent backflow of blood into the atria during ventricular contraction
C Produce serous fluid for lubrication
D Store calcium ions for muscle contraction
Explanation
The chordae tendineae and papillary muscles work together to prevent the AV valve flaps from everting into the atria during ventricular contraction, thus preventing backflow of blood.
Question 15 of 15
Which of the following is responsible for the 'lub' sound in the heart sounds?
A Closing of the semilunar valves
B Closing of the atrioventricular valves
C Opening of the semilunar valves
D Opening of the atrioventricular valves
Explanation
The 'lub' sound (S1) is caused by the closing of the atrioventricular valves (tricuspid and mitral) at the beginning of ventricular systole.
