Question 1 of 15
Which of the following is NOT one of the main functions of the urinary system?
A Excretion
B Elimination
C Digestion
D Homeostasis
Explanation
The urinary system performs three main functions: excretion (removal of waste), elimination (discharge of waste), and homeostasis (regulation of blood plasma volume, solutes, and pH). Digestion is not a function of the urinary system.
Question 2 of 15
Which of the following is the correct order of the protective layers surrounding the kidney from outermost to innermost?
A Fibrous capsule → Adipose capsule → Renal fascia
B Adipose capsule → Renal fascia → Fibrous capsule
C Renal fascia → Adipose capsule → Fibrous capsule
D Renal fascia → Fibrous capsule → Adipose capsule
Explanation
The kidneys are protected by three layers from outermost to innermost: renal fascia (anchors kidney), adipose capsule (cushions against blows), and fibrous capsule (protection from infections).
Question 3 of 15
What is the structural and functional unit of the kidney?
A Glomerulus
B Nephron
C Collecting duct
D Renal corpuscle
Explanation
The nephron is the structural and functional unit of the kidney. There are approximately 1 million nephrons in each kidney, and they carry out the processes that form urine.
Question 4 of 15
Which part of the nephron is primarily responsible for reabsorption of water, glucose, amino acids, and ions?
A Distal convoluted tubule
B Loop of Henle
C Proximal convoluted tubule
D Collecting duct
Explanation
The proximal convoluted tubule (PCT) is primarily responsible for reabsorption of water, glucose, amino acids, and ions. It has microvilli that increase its surface area for absorption.
Question 5 of 15
What is the name of the triangular region in the urinary bladder formed by the openings of the ureters and the urethra?
A Rugae
B Detrusor
C Trigone
D Hilum
Explanation
The trigone is a triangular region in the urinary bladder formed by the openings of the two ureters and the urethra. It is clinically important as infections tend to persist in this area.
Question 6 of 15
Which process in urine formation involves the movement of substances from the blood into the filtrate?
A Glomerular filtration
B Tubular reabsorption
C Tubular secretion
D Osmosis
Explanation
Tubular secretion is the process where substances (such as H+, K+, creatinine, and drugs) are moved from the blood in the peritubular capillaries into the filtrate in the renal tubules.
Question 7 of 15
What specialized cells in the glomerular capsule have foot-like processes that form filtration slits?
A Mesangial cells
B Podocytes
C Juxtaglomerular cells
D Macula densa cells
Explanation
Podocytes are specialized cells in the visceral layer of the glomerular capsule that have foot-like processes (pedicels) that form filtration slits, creating part of the filtration membrane.
Question 8 of 15
Which hormone increases water reabsorption in the collecting ducts by making them more permeable to water?
A Aldosterone
B Antidiuretic hormone (ADH)
C Renin
D Erythropoietin
Explanation
Antidiuretic hormone (ADH) increases water reabsorption in the collecting ducts by making them more permeable to water, resulting in concentrated urine and reduced urine volume.
Question 9 of 15
What is the normal pH range of urine?
A 2.0-4.0
B 4.5-8.0
C 7.0-7.4
D 8.5-9.0
Explanation
The normal pH range of urine is 4.5-8.0, with an average of about 6.0. Diet can influence urine pH, with protein-rich diets producing more acidic urine and vegetarian diets producing more alkaline urine.
Question 10 of 15
Which of the following is NOT a nitrogenous waste product found in urine?
A Urea
B Uric acid
C Creatinine
D Glucose
Explanation
The main nitrogenous waste products in urine are urea (from amino acid breakdown), uric acid (from nucleic acid breakdown), and creatinine (from muscle metabolism). Glucose is not normally present in urine and its presence indicates glycosuria, often associated with diabetes.
Question 11 of 15
What is the term for the involuntary smooth muscle sphincter that controls the flow of urine from the bladder to the urethra?
A External urethral sphincter
B Internal urethral sphincter
C Detrusor muscle
D Trigone
Explanation
The internal urethral sphincter is an involuntary smooth muscle that controls the flow of urine from the bladder to the urethra. The external urethral sphincter is voluntary skeletal muscle that provides conscious control over urination.
Question 12 of 15
Which hormone promotes sodium reabsorption and potassium secretion in the distal convoluted tubule and collecting duct?
A Antidiuretic hormone (ADH)
B Aldosterone
C Renin
D Erythropoietin
Explanation
Aldosterone promotes sodium reabsorption and potassium secretion in the distal convoluted tubule and collecting duct. This helps regulate blood pressure and electrolyte balance.
Question 13 of 15
At approximately what volume of urine in the bladder is the urge to urinate typically felt?
A 50 mL
B 100 mL
C 200 mL
D 500 mL
Explanation
The urge to urinate (micturition) is typically felt when the bladder contains about 200 mL of urine. Stretch receptors in the bladder wall are activated at this volume.
Question 14 of 15
Which of the following conditions is characterized by the presence of glucose in the urine?
A Proteinuria
B Hematuria
C Glycosuria
D Ketonuria
Explanation
Glycosuria is the condition characterized by the presence of glucose in the urine. This can occur in diabetes mellitus when blood glucose levels exceed the renal threshold for reabsorption.
Question 15 of 15
What is the name of the smooth muscle in the wall of the urinary bladder that contracts during urination?
A Trigone
B Detrusor
C Rugae
D Sphincter
Explanation
The detrusor muscle is the smooth muscle in the wall of the urinary bladder that contracts during urination to expel urine. When it contracts, it increases pressure within the bladder.
