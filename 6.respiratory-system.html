Question 1 of 15
Which structures are part of the Upper Respiratory System?
The upper respiratory structures include all major respiratory passageways: nose, sinuses, pharynx, larynx, and trachea.
Question 2 of 15
What are the three divisions of the pharynx?
The pharynx is divided into three regions: nasopharynx, oropharynx, and laryngopharynx.
Question 3 of 15
What is the primary function of the epiglottis?
During swallowing, the epiglottis closes off the laryngeal opening to prevent food from entering the airway.
Question 4 of 15
How many lobes does the right lung have?
The right lung is tri-lobed (has three lobes), while the left lung is bi-lobed (has two lobes).
Question 5 of 15
What is the function of pleural fluid?
Pleural fluid is secreted into the pleural cavity and provides lubrication against friction during lung expansion.
Question 6 of 15
What are the four processes required for respiration?
The four processes are: 1) Pulmonary Ventilation, 2) External Respiration, 3) Internal Respiration, and 4) Transport of Respiratory Gases.
Question 7 of 15
According to Boyle's Law, what happens when pressure increases?
Boyle's Law states that pressure varies inversely with volume. As pressure increases, volume decreases.
Question 8 of 15
What primarily drives inspiration (inhalation)?
Inspiration depends on the action of the diaphragm (pulling down on contraction) and intercostal muscles (pulling sternum forward), which increase thoracic volume.
Question 9 of 15
In a healthy male, what is the approximate surface area of the respiratory membrane?
In healthy male lungs, the respiratory membrane has a surface area of approximately 50-70 m¬≤, allowing for efficient gas exchange.
Question 10 of 15
What percentage of oxygen is transported bound to hemoglobin?
Oxygen is transported 98.5% bound to hemoglobin and only 1.5% dissolved in plasma.

What percentage of carbon dioxide is transported as bicarbonate ions in plasma?

Question 13 of 15
What happens in emphysema that affects gas exchange?
In emphysema, alveoli burst and merge, reducing the surface area available for diffusion and gas exchange.
Question 14 of 15
What is the term for the space that never aids in gas exchange?
Dead space refers to areas that never aid in gas exchange, including anatomical dead space (air conducting zones) and alveolar dead space (non-functional alveoli).
Question 15 of 15
What is produced by Type II alveolar cells to aid in diffusion?
Type II alveolar cells secrete surfactant, which creates a moist diffusion surface and reduces surface tension in the alveoli.
